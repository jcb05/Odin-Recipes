Cheesecake

Directions

Prepare yourself for a delightful journey into the world of cheesecake with this simple yet irresistible recipe. Begin by creating a buttery graham cracker crust, blending crushed crackers with melted butter and a hint of sugar, then pressing firmly into a springform pan. Whip together cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth, adding eggs one at a time until incorporated. Pour this creamy mixture over the crust and bake until the edges are set with a slight jiggle in the center. After cooling and refrigerating, serve this velvety cheesecake chilled, perhaps adorned with fresh berries or a drizzle of caramel, for a dessert that promises pure indulgence in every forkful.

Ingredients

    For the crust: 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs (about 10-12 whole graham crackers) 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted For the filling: 4 packages (8 ounces each) cream cheese, softened 1 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 4 large eggs, at room temperature Optional additions: 1/2 cup sour cream (for added richness, optional) 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (helps to bind the filling and prevent cracking) Pinch of salt For garnish (optional): Fresh berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries) Fruit compote (such as raspberry or cherry) Chocolate ganache Whipped cream

Steps

    Prepare the Crust: Preheat your oven to 325째F (160째C). In a food processor or by hand, crush graham crackers into fine crumbs. Mix the graham cracker crumbs with melted butter and sugar until well combined. Press the mixture firmly and evenly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan to form the crust. Use the bottom of a glass or measuring cup to help pack it down tightly. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 10 minutes. Remove and set aside to cool while preparing the filling. Make the Filling: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy. Add granulated sugar and beat until incorporated and fluffy. Mix in vanilla extract (or other flavorings like lemon zest) until combined. Add eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed just until each egg is incorporated. Be careful not to overmix, as this can incorporate too much air and lead to cracks in the cheesecake. If using, stir in sour cream and flour until just combined. Sour cream adds richness and flour helps to stabilize the filling. Pour the filling over the prepared crust in the springform pan. Smooth the top with a spatula. Bake the Cheesecake: Place the filled springform pan on a larger baking sheet to catch any drips. Bake in the preheated oven at 325째F (160째C) for about 45-55 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center still jiggles slightly when shaken. Turn off the oven and crack the oven door open slightly. Let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour to prevent cracking. Chill the Cheesecake: Remove the cheesecake from the oven and run a knife around the edge of the pan to loosen the cheesecake from the sides. Allow the cheesecake to cool completely at room temperature, then cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to set and chill thoroughly. Serve and Garnish: When ready to serve, remove the cheesecake from the springform pan by releasing the latch on the side. Transfer the cheesecake to a serving plate and garnish as desired with fresh berries, fruit compote, chocolate ganache, or whipped cream.
